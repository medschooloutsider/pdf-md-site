# Neurobiology Reading Pack

## Section 4. Synaptic Transmission

The synapse is the junction where one neuron communicates with another cell.
Electrical signals arriving at the presynaptic terminal trigger neurotransmitter release.
Those molecules cross the synaptic cleft and bind receptors on the postsynaptic membrane.

### Key ideas

- information transfer depends on release, diffusion, and receptor binding
- synapses can be excitatory or inhibitory
- synaptic strength can change over time
